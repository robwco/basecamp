project #5: charm’s tabs

since everything so far has been really high-level, i thought i'd close with a closer look at a specific design problem that i worked on during the month we updated charm…

before

the previous version of the app had a fun and distinctive navigation system but presented some usability problems:

  • using these button/tabs is confusing because you expect a button labeled "Process" to actually process something when clicked. it instead takes you to the process page.
  • it also doesn't indicate where you are inside the app very well. the triangle indicator below the tab looks like a tooltip instead of a tab.

my goal was to keep the feel of the colors and tabs but fix the usability problems they presented.

after

it was a big challenge to redesign these tabs and took a long time to come to a solution. almost everything i tried either lost the distinctive colors or presented new usability problems. the solution was to:

  • rename the process tab to “Inbox” which makes it more clear you’re not taking an action but instead going somewhere.
  • close the triangle to indicate which tab you’re on, and instead use shading and hover animations.
